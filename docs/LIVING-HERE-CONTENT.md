helpful local sharing tips that have helped generations — warm, practical, never smug or nose-rubbing.

Labor Day, propane topped off before the first storm, know your neighbors, keep a paper map) — presented
as friendly wisdom anchored to the verified facts, NEVER as invented local anecdotes. Each page: short
intro, the facts with sources, an "Old-timer's rule of thumb" callout or two (generic mountain wisdom

## 1. ❄️ Winter & Access (`winter-access.html`)
- **Plowing (county primary source, road-by-road):** CR 501 = **Level 1** priority ("continuous
  all-weather access," plowed before the school buses run); CR 500/501A/502 = Level 2 (same-day target,
  first delayed in big storms). District V shop (Bayfield) (970) 382-6420. Gravel roads plowed after 4".
  Src: lpcgov.org/departments/road_and_bridge/county_snow_removal_priorities_and_practices.php
- **Snowfall honesty:** ~**85 in/yr at the dam** (NOAA 1991–2020 normals, station USC00058582) — about
  double Durango; the high country gets several times that (the 200+ inch figures belong to the
  Weminuche SNOTEL, not the lake). Src: NCEI normals.
- **Traction law applies to US 160/550 only** (state highways), not CR 501. Since fall 2025: AWD/4WD with
  3/16" winter-rated tread OR chains/ATD when activated. Fines: say "roughly $50–$130; $650+ if you
  block the road" (CDOT's own pages conflict on exact figures). Src: codot.gov tractionlaw + Nov 2025
  release.
- **Power:** LPEA co-op; live outage map outage.lpea.coop; winter outages happen (documented Herald
  coverage "north of Bayfield") — generator/wood-heat readiness framing.
- **Heat:** no routine indoor-burning bans here; new stoves must be EPA-2015-compliant (CDPHE).
  **Outdoor burning needs a FREE 1-year UPRFPD burn permit** (issued 7 days/wk incl. Station 4 North
  Vallecito, (970) 444-0030); permits auto-suspend on Red Flag days. Src: upperpinefpd.org.
- **Alerts:** LPC Alerts! signup (see corrections). Old-way callout: "save the alert number in your
  phone like the neighbors' — a 2 a.m. call you answer."
- Old-timer's rules: woodshed full by Labor Day; keep the tank (gas AND propane) above half from
  Halloween on; a shovel, blanket, and headlamp live in the truck all winter.
- Cross-links: conditions (live roads/weather), firewise (Red Flag), Nordic club winter trails.

## 4. 🔥 Firewise Living (`firewise-living.html` — expand the existing page)
- **CSFS Home Ignition Zones:** Zone 1 (0–5 ft) NOTHING combustible — gravel not mulch, no woodpile on
  the porch; Zone 2 (5–30 ft) spaced/limbed; Zone 3 (30–100 ft) thinning. Link the 2021 CSFS HIZ guide
  PDF. Src: csfs.colostate.edu.
- **Wildfire Adapted Partnership** (Durango 501(c)(3)): FREE Wildfire Risk Site Visit → unlocks
  **Defensible Space Cost-Share** reimbursement; **chipper rebate**; Neighborhood Ambassador program
  (150+ volunteers). A FAC-network article describes **monthly summer slash-depot days at Vallecito**
  run by a local ambassador (1-src — publish as "WAP has organized slash days at the lake; check
  wildfireadapted.org/calendar"; DAVID-CONFIRM 2026 dates). Src: wildfireadapted.org.
- **Vallecito has its own CWPP (Nov 2014)** hosted by UPRFPD — better local cite than Forest Lakes':
  upperpinefpd.org/wp-content/uploads/2022/05/Vallecito_CWPP.pdf
- **Insurance:** the **Colorado FAIR Plan opened April 2025** — last resort (3 denials required, $750k
  cap, actual-cash-value): "a backstop, not a plan." **HB25-1182** requires insurers to credit
  documented mitigation. Src: coloradosun/cpr + leg.colorado.gov + doi.colorado.gov.
- **LPC Alerts! + Ready-Set-Go** evacuation framing; one paved route reality already on the site;
  county Wildfire Resource Center lpcgov.org/livingwithfire.php. Coronado CFM tie-in (profiled claims
  only). Do NOT claim Firewise USA recognized-site status (unverified).
- Old-timer's rules: mitigation is spring chores, not a one-time project; the neighbor with the chipper
  is worth more than the insurance agent — be both.

## 5. 🏡 Buying Property (`buying-property.html`)
- **Wells:** <35 acres = household-use-ONLY permit (indoor water; no lawn, no livestock); 35+ acres =
  domestic well (3 dwellings, 1 acre irrigation). Check the permit class BEFORE falling for the pasture.
  Cisterns/hauled water exist (LAPLAWD runs a bulk fill station) — keep generic. Src: DWR Guideline
  2023-1; CSU Extension 6.700. State doesn't test private wells — you do (CDPHE).
- **Septic:** mandatory third-party inspection at sale → **La Plata County Public Health Acceptance
  Document ($110, valid 12 months)**; systems finaled within 4 years exempt. Mirror the county page's
  current program wording at build (TOT → "Continued Use Permit" rename is 1-src). Src: lpcgov.org
  septic page + TOTI Guide PDF.
- **Access:** county maintains 653 mi of roads but NEVER private roads (and restricts accepting new
  ones) — demand the recorded road-maintenance agreement + who plows. Winter access is a cost line.
- **Insurance:** bind early — WUI underwriting is slow/picky; FAIR Plan is the floor not the goal;
  HB25-1182 makes documented mitigation count.
- **Taxes:** pleasantly low — "roughly 6–7% residential assessment rate set by state law (SB24-233)" +
  link the assessor (do NOT print an exact % — sources conflict; re-check at build).
- **Title:** "confirm in title work whether the lot is fee-simple or sits on leased land — the
  reservoir itself is on federal Reclamation project land." (Whether ANY lake cabins are on leased lots
  is UNVERIFIED either way — DAVID/title-company question; publish nothing stronger.)
- STR: lodging tax + Good Neighbor Policy (cross-link respect-vallecito). Julie = the site's
  real-estate resource (profiled claims only).
- Old-timer's rules: buy the road and the water before you buy the view; visit the lot in February
  before you close in June.
