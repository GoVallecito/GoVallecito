## Lead facts (open with 2–3 of these)
1. **La Plata County leads Colorado in bear deaths and relocations** — 2024: 14% of all bears euthanized
   and 25% of all relocations statewide happened in this one county. (Bear Smart Durango 2024 recap)
2. **Almost every bear conflict here is preventable and human-caused** — trash is the #1 attractant; a
   bear's nose smells food up to **5 miles** away and in fall it eats ~22 hrs/day for ~20,000 calories.
   (CPW)
3. **Moose — not bears — are the most dangerous animal to encounter, and dogs are the trigger.** CPW:
   moose are "one of the more dangerous animals we have… not scared of anything," move up to 35 mph;
   unleashed dogs are "by far the most prevalent instigation for moose attacks in Colorado." They've
   expanded into the Weminuche above the lake.
4. **"A candy bar or empty soda can is all it takes"** to total your car. (Bear Smart Durango)
5. Feeding wildlife is illegal ($100, CPW) and leaving trash/feeders out violates state law + the 2008
   La Plata County ordinance — the law targets negligence, not just intent.

## Bear Smart Durango (the "who to trust" block)
Local nonprofit (founded 2003; director **Bryan Peterson**) working to reduce human-bear conflict in
the Durango/La Plata area — attractant removal (loaner bear-resistant cans, electric-fence incentives,
fruit gleaning, bird-feeder exchanges), education, and the policy work behind the city/county bear-trash
laws. They helped launch the **La Plata County Bear Hotline (970) 247-BEAR (2327)**. Link
bearsmartdurango.org; "$100 rebate toward a bear-resistant container." (Don't state a 501(c)(3) class —
unverified; say "local nonprofit." Src: bearsmartdurango.org/about, /be-bear-smart, /2024recap.)

## Species blocks — each = one headline behavior change
1. **🐻 Black bears** — *Lock up every scent.* Trash out only the MORNING of pickup (never overnight),
   nothing scented in cars or tents, remove bird feeders while bears are out (a 7-lb seed tube =
   12,000+ cal), pick fruit early, clean grills, feed pets indoors. Encounter (CPW): don't run or climb;
   stand ground, yell, throw things; bear spray ~40 ft; **if attacked, FIGHT BACK — do not play dead**
   (black bears, not grizzlies). "A fed bear is a dead bear": CPW's **two-strike** policy — a tagged
   nuisance bear relocated once; handled again, euthanized. Src: cpw.state.co.us/living-bears + BSD.
2. **🫎 Moose** — *Leash the dog and give them a very wide berth.* The most dangerous large animal here;
   dogs trigger attacks (moose see them as wolves). Extra space for cows with calves and in willow
   thickets. Src: cpw.state.co.us/living-moose.
3. **🦁 Mountain lions** (rare) — *Never run — look big and fight back if attacked.* Speak calmly, back
   away, raise arms/open jacket, pick up kids/pets, throw things. Never approach a lion feeding or with
   kittens. Src: cpw.state.co.us/living-mountain-lions.
4. **🦌 Elk & deer** — *Slow down at dawn and dusk*, especially spring/fall migration, on CR 501. Most
   collisions happen then. Never touch fawns/calves in spring — the mother is near. Src:
   cpw.state.co.us/wildlife-collisions + /dont-rescue-young-wildlife.
5. **🦅 Osprey & bald eagles** (protected) — *Keep your distance from nests.* Osprey nest at the lake;
   both federally protected. Colorado raptor-buffer guidance: ~⅛ mi (≈660 ft) from osprey nests, ½ mi
   from active bald-eagle nests (Oct 15–Jul 31). Use **non-lead tackle** (frame as best practice to
   protect eagles/loons, NOT a local rule — unverified locally). Src: CPW raptor buffer guidelines;
   USFWS eagle management.
6. **🐍 Rattlesnakes** — *A lower-elevation concern, not a lakeshore one.* Near their upper limit at
   ~7,700 ft; far more likely on warm canyon hikes down toward Bayfield/lower Pine River. "Watch your
   step on lower hikes," don't overstate. Src: cpw.state.co.us/species/prairie-rattlesnake.
7. **🦝 Raccoons, skunks & camp scavengers** — *Secure food and coolers* (same rules as bears; fold into
   the camping note). Src: cpw.state.co.us/protect-your-campsite-wildlife.
8. **🐄 Open-range cattle** — *Expect cattle on forest roads; slow down, pass calmly, don't haze.* The
   San Juan NF is actively grazed (Columbine RD, incl. the FR 724/Pine River area). Frame as
   courtesy/safety, not a cited right-of-way rule. Src: fs.usda.gov/r02/sanjuan/natural-resources/range.

## Hosts / vacation-rental box (ties to STR Good Neighbor angle)
Bear Smart's vacation-rental guidance: bear-resistant cans with lids LOCKED, doors/windows/garage/cars
closed + locked, nothing scented in vehicles, pets fed indoors, clean grills, report activity to the
hotline. "Print Bear Smart's visitor flyer and leave it for your guests" (bearsmartdurango.org visitors
flyer PDF). Also CPW BearWise basics (bearwise.org).
