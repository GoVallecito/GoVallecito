# "Respect Vallecito" — verified content source (June 2026)

Research record for respect-vallecito.html. Every fact carries a source; the "publish-safety" flags are
binding (same verify-or-omit discipline as the rest of the site). Tone: confident, cooperative, "we"
voice — celebration first, stewardship as belonging, never scolding. Addressed to visitors AND
full-timers. The words "Airbnb" and "tourists" never appear pejoratively.

## The wow material (build the page around these — all verified)
1. **Colorado's largest Wilderness is the backyard** — the Weminuche: 499,771 acres ("three quarters the
   size of Rhode Island" — FS's phrase), designated 1975, expanded 1980/1993; 50 miles of the
   Continental Divide; three 14ers (Eolus, Sunlight, Windom); average elevation 10,000 ft. Headwaters of
   BOTH the Rio Grande and San Juan rivers — "providing clear drinking water to millions" (FS, quotable).
   Src: fs.usda.gov/r02/sanjuan/recreation/weminuche-wilderness · wilderness.net/?ID=637
2. **Two of its great gateways start at this lake** — Vallecito Creek #529 (campground → Continental
   Divide, ~18 mi) and Pine River #523 ("some of the finest fly-fishing anywhere" — FS).
3. **The water carries an 1868 priority date** — the dam exists partly to honor Ute treaty water rights;
   the **Southern Ute Indian Tribe owns a one-sixth interest in Vallecito Reservoir** (confirmed by
   Congress, 1988 settlement). Src: pineriverirrigationdistrict.com/pine-river-project-history
4. **A New Deal lake** — Bureau of Reclamation, built 1938–1941; WPA workers + CCC enrollees cleared
   ~5 million board feet of timber from the valley floor before the water rose; ranchers, farmers, and
   Utes lobbied for it together. Src: PRID history · livingnewdeal.org · coloradoccc.org
5. **The bighorns above Vallecito Creek are originals** — a Tier 1, genetically distinct NATIVE herd
   that never died out; only ~425 animals remain across three herds. Src: mountainstudies.org/bighorndetails
6. **The cats came back** — 218 Canada lynx released into the San Juans 1999–2007 (Colorado's most
   successful carnivore reintroduction); moose reintroduced from 1991 now wander the high drainages.
   Src: coloradovirtuallibrary.org lynx article · cpw.state.co.us moose page
7. **Osprey are "plentiful," bald eagles cruise the shoreline** — official Colorado Birding Trail site;
   loons and goldeneye in migration. Src: coloradobirdingtrail.com/site/vallecito-reservoir/
8. **A state-record fishery** — CPW: Vallecito "produced a state record northern pike," trophy pike
   routinely; brown trout to 30"/14 lbs. Src: cpw.state.co.us/body-of-water/vallecito-reservoir
9. **After the 2002 Missionary Ridge Fire** (70,000+ acres, 39 days, firefighter Alan Wyatt killed),
   the community answered with art: the **Tour of Carvings — 14 sculptures, ~18 ft, ~2,600 lbs each**,
   carved from fire-killed trees by Chad Haspels, each crowned with a flame. Src: carvingsatvallecito.org
10. **The valley keeps being written by water** — Oct 2025: remnants of a Pacific tropical storm pushed
    Vallecito Creek to ~7,000 cfs (double any rainfall on record for those dates); ~350 homes evacuated;
    the community is rebuilding again. Src: Durango Herald flood coverage.
- Community scale: 400–500 full-time residents, ~2,000 in summer; "surrounded by over 2.5 million acres
  of public land" (durango.org fact sheet — official DMO, cite it).

## ⚠️ Accuracy flags (binding)
- NEVER translate "Weminuche" (no verified etymology). Say "named for the Weminuche band of the Ute people."
- Ute bands: the namesake Weeminuche band is today the **Ute Mountain Ute Tribe**; the reservation just
  south (Ignacio, on this river) is the **Southern Ute Tribe** (Mouache + Capote). Phrase carefully —
  "the Ute bands of the Pine River country" is safe.
- NO dark-sky certification exists — "genuinely dark skies," not "Dark Sky Park."
- Dam construction: "1938–1941."

## The stewardship asks — each grounded in verified law (rule → source → publish-safety)
1. **Slow down on CR 501/500.** Mountain county roads; kids, dogs, deer, cyclists. Sheriff patrols the
   lake (PRID rec fees help fund it). DON'T publish a specific speed number (CR 501 posted limits not
   verifiable online) — "obey posted limits; they're slower than you think for a reason."
2. **OHVs/side-by-sides don't belong on the county roads — it's state law, not local grumpiness.**
   C.R.S. 33-14.5-108: illegal on any public road unless the county designated it open — **La Plata
   County has designated none** (verified by absence on Stay The Trail's statewide list; soften to "no
   La Plata County road has been designated open to OHVs"). Crossings only. The legal riding is on San
   Juan NF routes per the MVUM (link), with CPW registration ($26.25). Fines: $50 road operation, $100
   undesignated federal route ($200 in Wilderness). Src: colorado.public.law/statutes/crs_33-14.5-108 ·
   staythetrail.org/ohvs-on-streets-and-roads/ · csp.colorado.gov press release.
3. **Kids on machines:** ages 10–15 may ride on designated trails ONLY under direct (visual) supervision
   of a licensed driver; 16+ independently; never on roads (see #2). Helmets: publish as guidance, not
   statute — "CPW and its education partners direct riders under 18 to wear DOT helmets." Src: CPW
   Chapter P-5 via cpw.state.co.us OHV page · staythetrail.org FAQs.
4. **Pack it out.** Littering = C.R.S. 18-4-511: mandatory fines $20–$500 first offense (to $1,000
   repeat), or court-ordered pickup hours; also prohibited on NF land (36 CFR 261.11). Frame positively:
   "the marina, market, and campgrounds all have dumpsters — there's no excuse we can't solve."
5. **Keep the volume down — sound carries on water.** USFS Vallecito CG quiet hours **10 pm–6 am**
   (generators limited); Colorado nuisance statute sets residential thresholds (55 dB day / 50 dB night,
   C.R.S. 25-12-103). Don't cite a county quiet-hour (none retrievable). Frame: "the loons and the
   neighbors both thank you."
6. **Fire is the one mistake this valley can't forgive.** Fireworks that leave the ground are ALWAYS
   illegal in Colorado; ALL fireworks are prohibited on National Forest land year-round; under sheriff
   fire restrictions violations run $500–$1,000 + suppression costs. Tie to the live restriction tile +
   Missionary Ridge. Src: dfpc.colorado.gov/fireworks · USFS fireworks page · 36 CFR 261.16(d).
7. **Leave the drones out of the Wilderness.** USFS: drones can't launch, land, or operate from
   designated Wilderness (motorized equipment/mechanical transport, 36 CFR 261.18). Over the lake and
   cabins: FAA rules + courtesy. No penalty figures. Src: fs.usda.gov UAS recreation page · wilderness.net.
8. **Don't feed the wildlife — secure the trash.** Feeding big game is illegal in Colorado ($100 fine,
   CPW; cite CPW page, NOT the new-law amounts — HB26-1342 just changed them and codified text lags).
   La Plata County requires bear-secure trash (ordinance exists; DON'T publish county fine amounts —
   unverified). A fed bear is a dead bear. Src: cpw.state.co.us/feeding-wildlife · 2 CCR 406-0 #021.
9. **If you rent your place (hosts):** unincorporated La Plata County requires no STR permit but DOES
   require lodging-tax collection; the county publishes a signed **Good Neighbor Policy** (noise,
   parking, trash, pets, fire) — "print it, frame it, leave it on the fridge for your guests." Src:
   lpcgov.org VRBO info page + Good Neighbor Policy PDF.
10. **On the water:** CPW boating rules apply (PFDs, careless operation, mufflers); ANS inspection at
    the ramp; PRID manages recreation. DON'T publish wake zones/quiet hours for the lake (PRID publishes
    none — David can ask PRID if he wants a line added). Src: cpw.state.co.us/activities/boating · PRID.
- **Full-timers too:** the same trash/bear, noise, burn-restriction, and MVUM rules apply year-round —
  one even-handed line so the page isn't visitor-vs-local.

## Tone guide (from David, refined)
- Confident and cooperative; "we" throughout ("we live here; we're glad you're here").
- Celebrate FIRST (the wow facts), then "how we keep it this way" — rules as shared standards with
  sources, not complaints. No shaming, no "those people," no anecdotes about bad behavior.
- Close on the carvings: this community has already rebuilt from fire and flood — "what we all do on an
  ordinary Tuesday is how it stays extraordinary."
