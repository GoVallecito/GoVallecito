# Vallecito-area trails — verified research record (June 2026)

Source for the trails page (Round 22). Multi-source facts unless marked **(1-src)**. Where sources
disagree, the range is given — publish the range or the FS figure. FS pages are on the NEW
`fs.usda.gov/r02/sanjuan/recreation/trails/<name>` scheme (old /recarea/ URLs 403).

## ⚠️ Standing cautions (publish prominently)
- **Oct 2025 flood damage:** FS (page updated Oct 22, 2025): **"Middle Bridge" ~5.5 mi up Vallecito
  Creek Trail washed away completely**; first bridge + twin bridges remain; forest-wide alert "multiple
  roads and trails damaged by flooding." Also the long-gone third bridge (~mile 7, avalanche 2004–05)
  = 40-ft ford, dangerous mid-May–mid-June runoff. Carry water shoes + poles.
- **Granite Peaks Ranch (Pine River TH):** trail starts through a private gate; first 2.7 mi cross
  ranch land — **no camping, no fishing, stay on trail, close gates**. Access is a fragile permissive
  easement (Hiking Project: "avoid actions which might encourage the ranch to close access").
- **Wilderness regs block** (all Weminuche trails): no bikes/motors/drones, groups ≤15 (people+stock
  ≤25), dogs under control, no camping within 100 ft of water. Columbine RD, Bayfield (970) 884-2512.
- **Emerald Lake camping closure:** no camping/fires within ¼ mi of Emerald + Little Emerald; no
  camping ½ mi north of Emerald.

## EASY
- **The Lake Trails / "Walk Path"** (no #) — signed segments along CR 501/CR 501A skirting the shore;
  flat; benches/picnic/fishing spots; the closest thing to a lakeshore promenade; on almost no apps.
  Src: durangotrails.org/trails/vallecito-lake-trails/the-lake-trails/
- **Stump Lake #662** (Lemon/Florida side) — 1.25 mi one-way, ~220 ft, 10,960→11,180 ft; small
  brook-trout lakes; kid-friendly high-lakes walk. Gatekeeper: FR 597 is rough/slow (high clearance).
  Src: fs.usda.gov …/trails/stump-lake-trail; hikingproject #662. (**Lost Lake** nearby: exists per FS
  Florida CG page; details unverified — mention without specifics.)
- **Tuckerville ghost-town walk** (FR 724 road-end, ~10 mi up) — easy high-country road walking to a
  1913–1928 copper-camp site (structures gone; look don't take); meadow/spruce ridge views. Road
  seasonal 6/1–11/30; passenger-car OK when dry to ~Runlett Park. Src: durangotrails Tuckerville page;
  La Plata Cty historic-tour PDF.
- **Dam-area walking** — CR 501 over the dam + south-end Walk Path segments. **No public trail below
  the dam** (private + Southern Ute land) — never promise a below-dam river walk. (Multi-src.)

## MODERATE
- **Lake Eileen #899** — THE under-used local hike. TH on CR 501 west side opposite the FS Work Center
  (park east side). FS: 2.1 mi one-way, ~1,150 ft (AllTrails 3.8 mi RT) — steep-for-its-length through
  aspen to a lily-pad lake + ridge overlook of the reservoir; fall-color standout; Colorado Birding
  Trail site; Wilderness rules apply. 681 AllTrails reviews. Src: FS lake-eileen-trail; durangotrails;
  alltrails.
- **Vallecito Creek #529 (day-hike portion)** — TH at Vallecito CG (end of CR 500); first 0.6 mi flat
  ("great with kids" — FS); switchbacks to ~500 ft above the creek; first bridge ~3 mi = classic
  turnaround. THE famous hike: AllTrails 4.8★ / ~1,644 reviews; busy first 3 mi. Winter snowshoe route.
  Src: FS vallecito-trail; hikingproject 7024158; durangotrails.
- **Pine River (Los Pinos) #523 — lower miles** — TH end of FR 602 (3.8 mi off CR 501, north end).
  Granite Peaks caution above. Wilderness boundary 2.7 mi; meadows + "some of the finest fly-fishing
  anywhere" (FS) above it. Full trail 23.2 mi to Weminuche Pass. Src: FS pine-river-trail; durangotrails.
- **North Canyon / East Creek #535 / Graham Creek** (east shore) — trio climbing creek drainages with
  small waterfalls to meadows; ~10.5 mi total system, ~3,340 ft (durangotrails — mostly 1-src);
  FS on East Creek: "lightly used… fly-fishermen cast for brook trout." Lightly maintained (deadfall,
  faint tread up high); horses share. TH access from FR 603 campgrounds / FR 852. Locals' trails.
- **Vallecito View #808** (FR 724 ~9.5 mi up) — 1.7 mi one-way, 10,670→11,380 ft; ridge viewpoint
  out-and-back; shared with OHV ≤50"; June 1–Nov 30. Src: FS vallecito-view-trail.
- **Red Creek #726** (Florida Rd side; NOT #607) — 3.5 mi, 8,000→9,600 ft to Missionary Ridge Trail;
  shaded creekside aspen/fir; 2002 burn-regeneration story; classic 10-mi loop w/ First Fork #727.
  Long season (Apr–Nov). Src: FS red-creek-trail; durangotrails; alltrails.

## HARD
- **Emerald Lake via Pine River + Lake Fork #528** — official name Lake Fork Trail; junction at Pine
  River mile ~6.3; ~10 mi one-way total, ~2,000–3,000 ft (AllTrails 20.2 mi OAB, Hard); Emerald +
  Little Emerald (largest natural lake in the Weminuche), option to Moon Lake (11,600). Camping
  closure above. Thaws late July. FS: "very popular" (by Weminuche standards). 469 AllTrails reviews.
  Src: FS lake-fork-trail; alltrails; durango.org.
- **Cave Basin #530** (FR 724, TH near mile 10, 10,800 ft) — FS 4.5 mi one-way (durangotrails 5.4),
  ~1,550–1,720 ft to 12,360 ft. Above treeline almost at once; karst limestone benches + sinkholes;
  rim overlook down to Dollar Lake and Emerald Lake 2,300 ft below — best non-technical view in the
  district. **Tread disappears — map/GPS required**; exposed to storms; July–Sept. Near-zero crowds.
  Src: FS cave-basin-trail; durangotrails; 10adventures.
- **Dark Canyon #814 → Runlett Park** (lower FR 724) — ~2 mi steep motorcycle-grade singletrack to the
  big Runlett Park meadow; surprising reservoir views; combine w/ ridge two-tracks toward Tuckerville.
  Figures 1-src (valhikes). Shared with moto; cattle.
- **Burnt Timber #667** (Transfer Park, Lemon side) — 7.0 mi, 8,500→11,600 ft, brutal first mile
  (~1,000 ft); huge aspen stands (fall sleeper); gateway to Lime Mesa/Mountain View Crest;
  Burnt Timber–Lime Mesa loop ~13 mi. Src: FS burnt-timber-trail; alltrails; Durango Herald.
- **Endlich Mesa #534** (end of FR 597, 11,280 ft) — historic sheep driveway; miles of open
  above-treeline mesa walking; ~8.5 mi one-way to City Reservoir, high point ~12,800 ft; easiest
  sustained alpine in the district ONCE past the drive — **FR 597 = 4WD/high-clearance, 1.5–2.5 hrs
  from Durango; no water on the mesa; storm exposure**. Do NOT publish the "Miller Mountain Rd from
  Vallecito CG" approach (1-src, conflicts with FS maps). Src: FS; climb13ers; CMC.
- **Shearer Creek #558** (CR 240 ~mile 12) — 7.8 mi unmaintained old stock driveway toward Missionary
  Ridge; solitude pick; **first ~2 mi cross private land — stay on trail, close every gate**; deadfall +
  faint tread. Src: hikearizona #558; trailforks; trip reports.

## STRENUOUS / OVERNIGHT (deep Weminuche — no road access to inner THs)
- **Vallecito Creek #529 full** — ~18–19.5 mi one-way, ~3,900–4,600 ft to Hunchback Pass/CDT (famously
  gentle grades; the fords are the crux — see flood caution). Junctions: Johnson Cr ~9 mi, Rock Cr
  ~15–17 mi (FS pages disagree — say "15–17"). 4–5-day classic. Trout in the creek.
- **Johnson Creek #821** (NOT #504 — #504 is Needle Creek on the Chicago Basin side; FS page updated
  Feb 2026 confirms #821) — from Vallecito jct mile ~9: 7.25 mi one-way, 9,100→12,680 ft (Columbine
  Pass); Columbine Lake 0.4 mi below the pass; the quiet back-door to Chicago Basin. July–Sept.
- **Rock Creek #655** — from Vallecito jct: 5.5 mi one-way to the Divide (12,330); Rock Lake (11,700)
  at ~4 mi (fair cutthroat; Flint Lakes good); meets Flint Creek #527 at ~5 mi. Centerpiece of the
  **Emerald–Vallecito loop (~36.6 mi)**. Moose/elk country.
- **Flint Creek #527** — Pine River mile ~12 ↔ Rock Creek; 8.4 mi, 9,240→12,000 ft past the Flint
  Lakes under the Pope's Nose. Genuinely obscure (2 ratings ever on Hiking Project).
