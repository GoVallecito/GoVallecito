Coronado Consulting & Fire Mitigation

"Small but mighty" — a women- and minority-owned wildfire-mitigation and tree-care company serving Bayfield, Vallecito, La Plata County, and Durango. Defensible space, hazard trees, and the planning and grant work behind them.

The short version

Two disciplines, one crew

Coronado Consulting & Fire Mitigation LLC was established in August 2019 and is owned and operated by Robert & Danielle Coronado (Mescalero Apache; women- and minority-owned). Robert brings roughly a decade as a wildland and structure firefighter, with a Type 2 initial-attack crew background, and leads the crews. Danielle is trained in emergency management — disaster prevention, preparedness, response, recovery, and mitigation — and runs operations and consulting. Together that means they can do the saw work and the planning, grants, and forest-management documents behind it.

Services

What they do

  • Wildfire mitigation & defensible space, including steep or limited-access terrain that can't be mechanically treated
  • Hazard-tree removal and storm/emergency response (24/7)
  • Tree health assessment and technical climbing
  • Tree thinning (certified sawyers), mastication, stump grinding, and wood chipping
  • Firewood and log sales
  • Consulting — grant writing, emergency management, forest-management plans, and Federal/State/Local/Tribal documentation

Free site assessments — they'll walk your property and email a bid.

Why it matters here

Fire is part of life in this valley

Coronado points to the conditions every property owner around the lake knows: drought, stressed and overgrown forests, and the bark-beetle epidemic killing stands of timber. Their flagship work has been mitigation in the Forest Lakes subdivision near Bayfield — described in the community wildfire protection plan as the largest subdivision in southwest Colorado (about 1,865 acres and 1,600+ parcels) and a top wildfire-risk area, modeled to burn through in roughly two hours — funded through wildfire-assistance and Colorado State Forest Service grants.

And the history is local: the 2002 Missionary Ridge Fire burned more than 70,000 acres right above Vallecito Lake — the reason defensible space is more than a talking point in this valley.

Missionary Ridge figures cited from independent fire history, not from the company.
