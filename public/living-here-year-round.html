Living in Vallecito · Living Here Year-Round

Living at the lake, year-round

Vallecito is a different place in February than in July. Here is the honest rhythm of the year and what full-time lake life actually involves.

The rhythm

The lake breathes in and out

Upper Pine River Fire serves about 15,000 people year-round, swelling past 30,000 in summer — and Vallecito is a big reason for the swing. At the lake itself, roughly 400–500 of us are full-timers (about 2,000 in summer). Winter is quiet, neighborly, and yours; summer is busy and bright. People who love it here love both halves. Sources: UPRFPD; Visit Durango.

🏔️ Old-timer's rule of thumb

Introduce yourself at the market and at the mailboxes. The winter neighbors are the real utility up here — you'll trade plowing, eggs, and a jump-start more than once.

The everyday

Mail, deliveries & school

Mail & packages: there's normal USPS delivery at the lake, and FedEx, UPS, and Amazon all deliver up here too — a genuine plus for full-time life. There's no Vallecito post office; the home office is Bayfield PO, 145 W Park St, (970) 884-9618 (Sat 10–noon), and every lake address is "Bayfield, CO 81122."

School: kids do get to school from up here. Bayfield School District 10 JT-R runs four schools (~1,250 students), and the published Bus Route 23 starts at the "Top of Vallecito" and runs down CR 501, serving all four schools. Transportation: (970) 884-5508. Source: bayfield.k12.co.us.

Who holds it together

The community backbone

  • Vallecito Conservation & Sporting Association (VCSA) — the nonprofit that wholly owns the marina; rental dollars feed lake conservation and the kids' fishing clinic (with PRID).
  • Upper Pine River Fire Protection District — your fire and ambulance service (more below).
  • Vallecito Church, the Nordic club, and PRID (which manages the reservoir and recreation) round out the civic life.

Fire & ambulance, in plain terms

Upper Pine River Fire (est. 1974, Chief Bruce Evans) runs 8 stations, 3 of them staffed 24 hours — including Station 4 (North Vallecito), 80 W. Vallecito Creek, with a daily minimum of two firefighters, and Station 5 (Forest Lakes). Station 2 at the dam is a resident-firefighter/seasonal-wildland station rather than 24/7. The district provides ALS ambulance transport (ambulances at Bayfield, Forest Lakes, and Vallecito; an 8-minute response goal at the lake, met about 80% of the time) and is CAAS-accredited — the only fire-based agency in Colorado with that accreditation, with a third straight perfect score in 2024. They also run swift-water and ice rescue. Source: UPRFPD.

One thing every resident should do: read the district's Vallecito wildland evacuation plan (a public PDF on the UPRFPD site) and sign up for LPC Alerts!. See the full emergency & lake contacts.

The calendar

Winter events & the social year

The winter highlights are real and worth circling: Winterfest is an annual community event, paired with the VCSA ice-fishing tournament in late February (running since 2015), and the Nordic ski swap in January. Beyond those, the calendar shifts year to year — so the honest advice is to watch the VCSA calendar and the bulletin board at the market rather than count on a fixed schedule.

🪵 Old-timer's rule of thumb

The lake teaches patience in May and gratitude in October. Show up to the winter events — that's where a seasonal place turns into a community.

Town & civics

County errands & voting

The bigger county business lives in Durango: the Clerk & Recorder and Motor Vehicle office is at 679 Turner Dr — and note it's closed Fridays. Colorado votes by mail ballot (register or check status at GoVoteColorado.gov), with drop boxes typically in Bayfield and Durango each cycle.

🏔️ Old-timer's rule of thumb

Do town in one trip, Monday through Thursday. Batch the errands, beat the Friday closures, and you've bought yourself a whole weekend at the lake.
