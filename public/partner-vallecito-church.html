Vallecito Church

A community church in the Vallecito valley, here since 1951, whose stated mission is to "encourage, equip, and evangelize the people of Vallecito valley." Sunday School at 9:00 AM, Worship at 10:30 AM — visitors are always welcome.

Who they are

A church for the valley

Vallecito Church is a community congregation with Southern Baptist heritage. Its senior pastor, Clark Cothern, was called in August 2024 and brings more than 30 years of ministry alongside his wife, Joy; the church is also served by elders Bob Tate, Rick McCune, and Ken Baker. Whether you're a longtime resident or up at the lake for a weekend, you're welcome to join a service.

The story

From a piano at Lost Creek Lodge

The church traces its roots to 1951, when neighbors gathered to sing around a piano at Lost Creek Lodge, hosted by Claude and Bunkie Crane. Singing grew into Bible study, and in 1958 the Vallecito Mission was born — sponsored by First Baptist Church of Ignacio, meeting under a 200-seat tent.

Ground was broken on August 16, 1959 for a 30×50 log church, completed in 1961. The congregation was formally constituted as Vallecito Baptist Church on August 1, 1971, with 26 charter members. An auditorium addition followed in 1975, and a Fellowship Hall expansion in 2021. Pastor Clark Cothern was called in August 2024. Through every chapter, as the church puts it, the spiritual goal has never changed: to spread the gospel throughout this valley.
