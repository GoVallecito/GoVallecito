Vallecito Church

A community of believers in the Vallecito valley, sharing the love of Jesus since 1951. Their welcome is simple: come as you are. Whether you live here year-round or are up at the lake for the weekend, there's a seat for you on Sunday.

Sundays

Join us on Sunday

Adult Bible Study — 9:00 AM
Worship Service — 10:30 AM

Visitors are always welcome, and there's no dress code — the focus here is on hearts, not appearances. On the second Sunday of each month, everyone is invited to stay for a potluck dinner after worship.

Who they are

A church for the valley

Vallecito Church is a community congregation with Southern Baptist heritage, serving the Bayfield and Vallecito valley area. Its senior pastor, Clark Cothern, was called in August 2024 and has served in ministry alongside his wife, Joy, for more than 30 years. He is supported by a team of elders — Bob Tate, Rick McCune, and Ken Baker — who help lead everything from children's ministry to the food pantry and firewood help for neighbors in need.

During the week & month

Other ways to gather

Alongside Sunday worship, the church family meets throughout the week:

  • Wednesday — Ladies' Bible Study, 10:00 AM (Fellowship Hall)
  • Wednesday — Evening Bible Study with Pastor Clark, 6:00 PM
  • Thursday — Men's Coffee, 8:00 AM at La Comida
  • First Saturday — Men's Breakfast, 9:00 AM
  • Second Sunday — Potluck dinner after worship
  • Third Sunday — Communion (the Lord's Supper)

Newcomers are welcome at any of these — bring a friend.

What they believe

Rooted in the gospel

Vallecito Church is a Bible-centered congregation that keeps the gospel of Jesus Christ at the heart of everything it does. It observes two ordinances — baptism by immersion and communion — as expressions of faith, and welcomes anyone seeking to know more. You can read the church's full Statement of Faith on its website.

The story

From a piano at Lost Creek Lodge

The church traces its roots to 1951, when neighbors gathered to sing around a piano at Lost Creek Lodge, hosted by Claude and Bunkie Crane. Singing grew into Bible study, and in 1958 the Vallecito Mission was born — sponsored by First Baptist Church of Ignacio, meeting under a 200-seat tent.

Ground was broken on August 16, 1959 for a 30×50 log church, completed in 1961. The congregation was formally constituted as Vallecito Baptist Church on August 1, 1971, with 26 charter members. An auditorium addition followed in 1975, and a Fellowship Hall expansion in 2021. Pastor Clark Cothern was called in August 2024. Through every chapter, as the church puts it, the spiritual goal has never changed: to spread the gospel throughout this valley.
