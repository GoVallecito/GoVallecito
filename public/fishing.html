Fishing

Six species, four seasons, one very good lake

Kokanee salmon are the headliner and a 20-pound-plus northern pike is the trophy. Here's what's biting, when, and what you need to fish legally.

🐟 Species

Plus brook trout up in the creek above the lake.

📅 Seasons

  • Ice-out (Apr–May) — pike and trout move shallow.
  • Summer (Jun–Aug) — kokanee trolling; the wind usually comes up by 2 p.m., so fish early.
  • Fall (Sep–Oct) — the best all-around fishing of the year.
  • Ice (Nov–Mar) — hard-water season; the tournament is late February.

🎫 Permits

  • Colorado fishing license — required, age 16+.
  • Habitat Stamp — $12.76, ages 18–64.
  • PRID recreation permit — for the reservoir; (970) 884-2558.

🗒️ Local notes

Fish early — the afternoon wind is real and predictable. Watch the lake level; when it drops, the fish reposition and some ramps get tricky. And during the fall kokanee run, know exactly where snagging is and isn't allowed.
