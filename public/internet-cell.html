Living in Vallecito · Internet & Cell Coverage

Staying connected at the lake

Coverage is patchy up here. Here is what actually works, where the dead zones are, and your options for home internet.

The headline

Fiber is being strung at the lake right now

The big news: Clearnetworx is running a roughly $13M grant-funded fiber build along CR 240 and CR 501 toward Forest Lakes and Vallecito — about 1,400 addresses, targeted for completion around summer 2026. As of this summer you can see it happening: cable going up on the overhead lines beside the lake. It's not lit at lake addresses yet, so the move is to check your address at clearnetworx.com. For reference, their Bayfield fiber runs $60/200 Mbps symmetrical, $80/500, $110/gig, no contracts or caps. Source: Clearnetworx; Durango Herald.

📶 Old-timer's rule of thumb

Don't cancel what works until the new line is actually lit and tested at your meter. Up here, "coming soon" and "working today" are two different seasons.

What works today

Home internet options now

  • Fixed wireless — Visionary Broadband lists Vallecito by name (vcn.com; they absorbed SkyWerx in 2017), and AlignTec serves La Plata County by free line-of-sight survey (aligntec.net). Both depend on a clear sightline to a tower.
  • Starlink — the reliable fallback in the canyons and the trees, and what a lot of full-timers run as their main or backup line.
  • CenturyLink DSL — legacy copper reaches some addresses; it works, but it's slow.

Tip: ask your prospective neighbors what they actually use — coverage here is address-by-address, sometimes tree-by-tree.

Cell, honestly

Cell coverage is spotty — plan for it

We won't oversell this. Coverage around the lake is patchy, with real dead zones on the CR 501 drive, and it's been documented the hard way — there was an eight-day AT&T outage at the lake covered in the local paper. A T-Mobile tower was approved at Forest Lakes, and there are recurring complaints in Verizon's footprint too. It's getting better, slowly, but the standing advice holds: download your maps offline and screenshot key info before you drive up. Signal boosters can help where there's a faint signal to amplify, but they can't make coverage from nothing.

📶 Old-timer's rule of thumb

The old way was the bulletin board at the market and a note on the fridge — and it still works. Print the directions, and tell someone where you're going. That habit predates cell phones for a reason.
