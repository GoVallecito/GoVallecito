Plan your visit

Everything you need before you point the truck up the hill

Vallecito is about 35 minutes from Durango, but it's a different world up here — higher, colder, and with one road in. A little planning goes a long way.

Getting here

How far is the lake?

🗓️ What's open, by season

Summer (Jun–Aug)

Peak season. Marina, restaurants, lodging, and guides are all running. Trailhead lots fill early; afternoon thunderstorms and wind are routine — get on the water and the trails in the morning.

Fall (Sep–Oct)

The locals' favorite: best fishing of the year, golden aspens, thinner crowds. Some seasonal businesses wind down after Labor Day, so call ahead.

Winter (Nov–Mar)

Quiet and cold. Cross-country skiing, snowshoeing, ice fishing, and the late-February ice-fishing tournament. Many services are seasonal; confirm before you come.

Spring (Apr–May)

Mud, runoff, and rising water. Ice-out fishing turns on. Trails up high are still snowed in — stick to lower routes.

⛽ Fuel & supplies

Top off in Bayfield or Durango on the way up, but you've got two solid options right at the lake:

  • Vallecito Marina — on-the-water fill-ups, the easy choice if your boat's already in.
  • Vallecito Lake Country Market — gas including non-ethanol fuel, ideal for boats, ATVs, and small engines that don't get along with ethanol. Best bet for trailered rigs and side-by-sides.

For groceries, ice, and basics, the Country Market and Rocky Mountain General Store have you covered. Cell service is spotty, so download maps and screenshot the emergency contacts before you lose signal.

🎒 Packing for the high country

  • Layers. It can be 80° at midday and near freezing at night, any month.
  • Sun protection. The sun is intense at altitude — hat, sunscreen, sunglasses.
  • Water. Altitude dehydrates you faster than you'd think. Drink more than usual.
  • Rain shell. Afternoon storms roll through fast in summer.
  • A full tank, snacks, and a plan B for when the trailhead lot is full.

🎫 Permits & passes

A PRID recreation permit is required for the reservoir (kayak, SUP, etc.) — (970) 884-2558. Fishing needs a Colorado license. Some winter Nordic areas charge a PRID land-use fee.
