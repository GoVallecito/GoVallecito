Vallecito Marina & Yacht Club

"The only marina on Vallecito Lake" — a full-service marina on what they call "the premier recreational lake in the Four Corners." Rent a boat, keep one here, fuel up, or grab tackle and a cold drink before you launch.

The short version

One marina, the whole lake

Vallecito is a big, high mountain reservoir, and there's just one marina on it. That makes the marina the natural jumping-off point for a day on the water — whether you're renting a pontoon for the family, taking out a fishing boat, or paddling a kayak along the shoreline. The marina is community-operated under the Vallecito Conservation and Sporting Association, with moorage run by agreement with the Bureau of Reclamation and the Pine River Irrigation District.

Best features

Why people start their day here

⛵ The only marina on the lake

Full-service and community-operated — fuel, store, slips, buoys, and rentals all in one place, right on the water.

🚤 A ready-to-go fleet

Five pontoon sizes plus a fishing boat and paddle craft. Powerboat rentals come with life jackets, a paddle, an anchor, a full tank of gas, and a landing net.

📅 Book online · flexible moorage

Reserve rentals online, and choose seasonal or short-term slips and buoys for your own boat.

Good to know

Before you go

  • Season: open 7 days a week, roughly May–September (weather and business permitting).
  • 2026 hours: Friday & Saturday 8 a.m.–6 p.m. · Sunday–Thursday 8 a.m.–5 p.m.
  • What's included with powerboats: life jackets, paddle, anchor, full tank of gas, landing net.
  • Dogs: welcome on pontoons; not on paddle craft.
  • Aquatic nuisance species: Vallecito is an inspection lake — see our First Visit guide for the clean-drain-dry rules before launching your own boat.

Note: "Yacht Club" is part of the marina's name — it's a public, community-operated marina, not a membership club with dues.
