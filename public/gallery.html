Vallecito in Pictures

The Ute homeland — the "little valley"

Vallecito — Spanish for "little valley" — lies in the ancestral homeland of the Ute (Weenuchiu / Weeminuche) people. These archival portraits show Ute and Southern Ute people of the era from federal and library collections; they are honest to that history, not claimed to be at Vallecito specifically.

The dam & the New Deal

Vallecito Dam was built 1938–1941 — a New Deal-era Bureau of Reclamation project on the Pine (Los Pinos) River that created the reservoir. Old Vallecito & early families

Ranching and homesteading families have worked the Pine River valley for generations. We're partnering with the Pine River Library to feature historic family photographs with permission; until then, this 1911 Forest Service scene shows the valley a century ago.
