Trails around Vallecito & into the Weminuche

From a flat lakeshore stroll to multi-day routes over the Continental Divide. Distances and trail numbers are from the San Juan National Forest; read the cautions below before you go.

Cautions Easy Moderate Hard Overnight
⚠️ Before you go — standing cautions

October 2025 flood damage

  • On Vallecito Creek #529, the "Middle Bridge" (~5.5 mi up) washed away completely — the first bridge and twin bridges remain. The long-gone third bridge (~mile 7) is a 40-ft ford, dangerous in mid-May–mid-June runoff. Carry water shoes + poles.
  • Forest-wide, "multiple roads and trails [were] damaged by flooding." Check current status with the Columbine Ranger District, (970) 884-2512.

Access & Wilderness rules

  • Pine River #523 starts through the private Granite Peaks Ranch gate; the first 2.7 mi cross ranch land — no camping, no fishing, stay on trail, close gates (it's a fragile permissive easement).
  • Weminuche Wilderness: no bikes/motors/drones, groups ≤15 (people+stock ≤25), dogs under control, no camping within 100 ft of water.
  • Moose & cattle: moose have moved into these drainages and unleashed dogs trigger charges — keep yours close; expect open-range cattle on forest roads. See the wildlife guide.
  • Emerald Lake: no camping/fires within ¼ mi of Emerald & Little Emerald; no camping ½ mi north of Emerald.

Check fire restrictions and live conditions before heading out; trailheads are on the lake map. And help keep the Weminuche wild — a quick read of Respect Vallecito covers the Wilderness rules (no drones, group size, camping setbacks).

🟢 Easy

The Lake Trails / "Walk Path"

Access: signed segments along CR 501/CR 501A. Flat, with benches, picnic and fishing spots — the closest thing to a lakeshore promenade, and on almost no apps. Great for families and a sunset stroll. durangotrails.org →

Stump Lake #662

1.25 mi one-way · ~220 ft (10,960→11,180 ft), Lemon/Florida side. A kid-friendly high-lakes walk to small brook-trout lakes. Getting there is the catch: FR 597 is rough and slow (high-clearance). Summer. San Juan NF →

Tuckerville ghost-town walk

Easy high-country road walking from the FR 724 road-end (~10 mi up) to a 1913–1928 copper-camp site — structures are gone (look, don't take), with meadow and spruce-ridge views. Road open seasonally ~Jun 1–Nov 30; passenger cars OK when dry to about Runlett Park. A genuine quiet pick. See the full Middle Mountain & Tuckerville guide. durangotrails.org →

Dam-area walking

Walk CR 501 over the dam and the south-end Walk Path segments. Note: there is no public trail below the dam (private + Southern Ute land) — don't count on a below-dam river walk.

🟡 Moderate

Lake Eileen #899

2.1 mi one-way · ~1,150 ft (FS; AllTrails 3.8 mi RT). The under-used local gem: steep-for-its-length through aspen to a lily-pad lake and a ridge overlook of the reservoir — a fall-color standout and a Colorado Birding Trail site. TH on the CR 501 west side opposite the FS Work Center (park on the east side). Wilderness rules apply. San Juan NF →

Vallecito Creek #529 (day-hike portion)

First bridge ~3 mi = the classic turnaround. TH at Vallecito Campground (end of CR 500). The first 0.6 mi is flat ("great with kids" — FS), then switchbacks climb ~500 ft above the creek. THE famous local hike (AllTrails 4.8★, ~1,644 reviews) and a winter snowshoe route — so the first 3 mi are busy. ⚠️ See the flood caution: the Middle Bridge (~5.5 mi) is gone. San Juan NF →

Pine River (Los Pinos) #523 — lower miles

TH at the end of FR 602 (3.8 mi off CR 501, north end). ⚠️ Crosses Granite Peaks Ranch for the first 2.7 mi to the Wilderness boundary (see cautions). Above it: open meadows and "some of the finest fly-fishing anywhere" (FS). The full trail runs 23.2 mi to Weminuche Pass. San Juan NF →

East-shore trio — North Canyon / East Creek #535 / Graham Creek

A quiet locals' system climbing the east-side creek drainages past small waterfalls to meadows — roughly 10.5 mi total, ~3,340 ft. FS on East Creek: "lightly used… fly-fishermen cast for brook trout." Lightly maintained (deadfall, faint tread up high); shared with horses. Access from the FR 603 campgrounds / FR 852. An insider pick. San Juan NF →

Vallecito View #808

1.7 mi one-way (10,670→11,380 ft) to a ridge viewpoint, off FR 724 (~9.5 mi up). Out-and-back; shared with OHVs ≤50". Season Jun 1–Nov 30. San Juan NF →

Red Creek #726

3.5 mi · 8,000→9,600 ft to the Missionary Ridge Trail (Florida Road side). Shaded creekside aspen and fir with a 2002-burn regeneration story; a classic 10-mi loop pairs it with First Fork #727. Long season (Apr–Nov). San Juan NF →

🟠 Hard

Emerald Lake — via Pine River + Lake Fork #528

~10 mi one-way · ~2,000–3,000 ft (AllTrails 20.2 mi out-and-back). The Lake Fork Trail branches off Pine River near mile 6.3 to Emerald Lake — the largest natural lake in the Weminuche — and Little Emerald, with an option on to Moon Lake (11,600 ft). "Very popular" by Weminuche standards; thaws late July. ⚠️ Camping closure within ¼ mi of the lake (see cautions). San Juan NF →

Cave Basin #530

4.5 mi one-way · ~1,550–1,720 ft to 12,360 ft, from a TH near mile 10 on FR 724. Above treeline almost at once: karst limestone benches and sinkholes, and a rim overlook 2,300 ft down to Dollar and Emerald lakes — the best non-technical view in the district. Tread disappears — map/GPS required; storm-exposed; July–Sept. Near-zero crowds. Access is up Middle Mountain (FR 724). San Juan NF →

Dark Canyon #814 → Runlett Park

~2 mi of steep, motorcycle-grade singletrack (lower FR 724) to the big Runlett Park meadow, with surprising reservoir views; combine with ridge two-tracks toward Tuckerville. Shared with motos; cattle country. durangotrails.org →

Burnt Timber #667

7.0 mi · 8,500→11,600 ft (Transfer Park, Lemon side) — a brutal first mile (~1,000 ft) into huge aspen stands (a fall sleeper) and the gateway to Lime Mesa / Mountain View Crest; the Burnt Timber–Lime Mesa loop is ~13 mi. San Juan NF →

Endlich Mesa #534

A historic sheep driveway and the easiest sustained alpine in the district once you're past the drive — miles of open above-treeline mesa to City Reservoir (~8.5 mi one-way, high point ~12,800 ft). The catch: FR 597 is 4WD/high-clearance, 1.5–2.5 hrs from Durango; no water on the mesa; full storm exposure. San Juan NF →

Shearer Creek #558

7.8 mi unmaintained old stock driveway toward Missionary Ridge (CR 240, ~mile 12) — a solitude pick with deadfall and faint tread. ⚠️ The first ~2 mi cross private land — stay on trail, close every gate. durangotrails.org →

🔴 Strenuous / overnight (deep Weminuche)

No road access to the inner trailheads — these start from Vallecito Creek or Pine River and go for days.

Vallecito Creek #529 — full

~18–19.5 mi one-way · ~3,900–4,600 ft to Hunchback Pass / the Continental Divide Trail — famously gentle grades; the fords are the crux (see flood caution). Junctions: Johnson Creek ~mile 9, Rock Creek ~mile 15–17 (FS pages disagree). A 4–5-day classic with trout in the creek. San Juan NF →

Johnson Creek #821

From the Vallecito junction (mile ~9): 7.25 mi · 9,100→12,680 ft to Columbine Pass, with Columbine Lake 0.4 mi below — the quiet back-door into Chicago Basin. July–Sept. San Juan NF →

Rock Creek #655

From the Vallecito junction: 5.5 mi to the Divide (12,330 ft), with Rock Lake (11,700 ft) at ~4 mi (fair cutthroat; the Flint Lakes fish well). The centerpiece of the ~36.6-mi Emerald–Vallecito loop; moose and elk country. durangotrails.org →

Flint Creek #527

Pine River mile ~12 to Rock Creek: 8.4 mi · 9,240→12,000 ft past the Flint Lakes under the Pope's Nose. Genuinely obscure — about as quiet as the Weminuche gets. durangotrails.org →
