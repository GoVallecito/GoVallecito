How we verify

Where our conditions come from

Every live number on this site is pulled automatically from a public agency feed every 15 minutes — we don't hand-type conditions, and we don't editorialize the data. Here's exactly what feeds what, and what we keep current by hand.

The pipeline

Automatic, every 15 minutes

A scheduled job fetches each source, normalizes it, and serves a single conditions file the site reads. If a source is down, we keep the last good reading and label it — we never invent a value or show a stale number as "now."

Automated vs. curated

What's a feed, and what's a person

🤖 Automated feeds

Weather, alerts, lake level, streamflow, wildfires, and roads are machine feeds from the agencies above. No human edits the numbers; the job just fetches and normalizes them every 15 minutes.

✍️ Locally curated

Fire-restriction stage, the business directory, emergency contacts, and the trip-planning guides are maintained by local contributors and updated as things change. We re-verify contacts before launch and fix mistakes fast.

Editorial policy

Independent & honest

  • Independent. GoVallecito.com is privately run and not affiliated with any government agency — not the National Weather Service, CDOT, the Forest Service, PRID, Colorado Parks & Wildlife, or any chamber of commerce. We collect and present their public information; they don't endorse us.
  • No pay-for-coverage. Businesses don't pay to be listed or to have their facts reported accurately — the directory is free.
  • Featured partners are labeled. A few local businesses sponsor the site to keep it free; they're clearly marked ★ Featured Partner. Sponsorship buys a richer listing — never favorable facts, hidden placement, or changed conditions data.
  • We link to primary sources. Every live number names its agency and the pipeline above shows exactly what feeds what — go straight to the source any time.
Corrections

Spot something wrong?

Email contact@govallecito.com (or use the contact form) — we correct errors promptly and note material corrections on the affected page. Whether it's a number that looks off, an out-of-date business detail, or a broken link, we want to know. We read everything.

Note: live conditions come straight from agency feeds. If a reading looks wrong it may be the agency's own value — when a feed is delayed we keep the last good reading and label it (stale) rather than show a number as "now."
