★ Featured partner · Fishing guide

Fisher Guide Service

"Open-water and ice-fishing charters" with Phil Fisher — the Vallecito Marina's go-to guide. Join him for a "unique, fun-filled fishing adventure" on Vallecito and Navajo Reservoir, with all the gear provided and the trip tailored to your group.

The short version

The lake's go-to guide

When folks at the marina get asked "who should I fish with?", the answer is Phil Fisher. Fisher Guide Service runs guided charters on Vallecito Reservoir — and over the ridge on Navajo Reservoir — for open-water season and again on the ice in winter. Phil's boat fishes up to four anglers, all the equipment and gear is provided, and every trip is tailored to what you and your group want to chase. New to the lake or a regular looking to get on this week's pattern, you book the boat and show up — he handles the rest.

Best features

Why book with Phil

🎯 Trips tailored to your group

"Uniquely tailored" charters — tell Phil what you want to catch and how you like to fish, and he builds the day around it. First-timers and seasoned anglers both welcome.

🗓️ Two reservoirs, all four seasons

Open-water charters on Vallecito and Navajo, plus winter ice-fishing once the lakes lock up. A guide who fishes year-round knows where they've moved.

🎣 Gear provided · up to 4 anglers

All the equipment and gear for the adventure is provided, and the boat fishes up to four people — bring your group, your license, and a cooler.

What he targets

Species on Vallecito

Vallecito holds a strong warm- and cold-water mix, and Phil chases all of it. See our fishing guide for seasons, regulations, and the month-by-month bite.

Walleye

The marquee fish — Vallecito is a well-known walleye fishery.

Northern pike

Big, toothy, and unlimited to harvest — a Phil specialty.

Smallmouth bass

Around the rocky structure once the water warms.

Trout — brown & rainbow

Stocked rainbows plus wild browns, year-round.

Kokanee salmon

A landlocked-salmon prize, best mid-to-late season.

Ice season & Navajo

Through the ice and over on Navajo, add perch, crappie, bluegill, largemouth, and catfish.

Good to know

Before you book

  • Seasons: open-water charters spring through fall; ice-fishing charters in winter (also Durango/Pagosa-area ice when conditions are right).
  • Group size: the boat fishes up to 4 anglers.
  • What's provided: all the equipment and gear for the trip.
  • What to bring: a valid Colorado fishing license, layers, food and drink, and sun protection. License and ANS details are in our First Visit and licenses & permits sections.
  • Booking: reach Phil through his Rates/Contact page, or call/text — see below.
