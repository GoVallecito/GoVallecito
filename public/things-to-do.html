How to spend a day (or a week) at Vallecito

Trails into the largest wilderness in Colorado, a 12-mile shoreline, horses, machines, chainsaw bears, and some of the darkest skies in the state — here's the local rundown.

🥾 Trails

Everything from a flat lakeside stroll to multi-day routes into the Weminuche Wilderness.

  • Lake Trail System — Easy. Gentle walking right along the water; good for families.
  • Vallecito Creek Trail — Moderate. 11.7 mi out-and-back, 1,456 ft gain. The classic.
  • Pine River Trail — Hard. 20.1 mi, 3,005 ft. Deep into the wilderness.
  • Rock Lake Trail — Strenuous. ~20 mi to an alpine lake.
  • Johnson Creek Trail — Moderate. ~8 mi, connects to the high country.

Pro tip: the Vallecito Creek trailhead lot fills by 9 a.m. on summer weekends. Get there early or have a plan B.

🚤 The lake

  • Boating — rentals at the Vallecito Marina.
  • Fishing — six species and four seasons; see the fishing guide.
  • Swimming — north end, no lifeguards, and it's snowmelt-cold well into summer.
  • Kayak / SUP — rent at the marina or bring your own; a PRID recreation permit is required.
  • Ice fishing — winter; the VCSA tournament is in February.

🐴 Horseback

  • Vallecito Lake Outfitters — guided rides and pack trips. (970) 759-9135.
  • Elk Point Lodge Stables — Thu–Mon, late May through early October.

🏍️ ATV & OHV

  • Altitude ATV & Side-by-Side Rentals — 32 Middle Mountain Rd · (970) 403-2260.
  • Grab a USFS Columbine Ranger District Motor Vehicle Use Map before you ride — not every road is open to OHVs.

🪓 Tour of Carvings

Fourteen Chad Haspels chainsaw sculptures around the lake, memorializing the 2002 Missionary Ridge Fire. Self-guided, free, year-round. Map and story at carvingsatvallecito.org.

❄️ Winter

  • Cross-country skiing — ~15 km groomed by the Vallecito Nordic Club, off FR 603 below Old Timers Campground (PRID land-use fee).
  • Snowshoeing — anywhere the snow's good.
  • Ice fishing — guided trips with Fisher Guide Service, (970) 769-0669.
  • Snowmobiling — on USFS roads.

🔭 Stargazing

Up at altitude with very little light pollution, Vallecito is a genuine dark-sky spot. Best viewing is September–November. Check the seeing forecast at ClearOutside before you go.
