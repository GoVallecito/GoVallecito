Living in Vallecito · Local Utilities & Services

Utilities & services up here

Most lake properties are on well and septic, and services look different than in town. Here is the practical rundown.

Power & propane

Electricity and propane country

Electricity is La Plata Electric Association, a member-owned co-op since 1939 — you get capital-credit refunds over time, and their outage map is the one to bookmark. Source: lpea.coop.

There are no gas mains up here — it's propane country. The suppliers locals rely on to run CR 501 reliably are Basin Co-op (Durango) and Ferrellgas (Bayfield). Get on a keep-full or auto-delivery plan and order ahead of cold snaps.

🪵 Old-timer's rule of thumb

Top off the propane before Halloween and never let it drop below about a third in January. The coldest week is exactly when the truck has the hardest time getting to you.

Water, septic & trash

Water, septic & trash

  • Water: there's no domestic water district at the lake — homes run on wells or cisterns (see the buying guide for permit classes). Bayfield's town system serves the town only.
  • Septic: nearly everyone is on a septic system, overseen by La Plata County Public Health. Budget to pump it every few years.
  • Trash & recycling: there's no county pickup, but WM (Waste Management) runs CR 501 — trash on Wednesdays, recycling every other week. The haul-it-yourself option is the Bayfield Transfer Station (4496 CR 223). Use bear-resistant cans either way — see the wildlife guide.
🏔️ Old-timer's rule of thumb

Know where your septic tank and leach field are before there's snow on the ground — and put the trash out the morning of pickup, never the night before. The bears keep a calendar too.

Health & town services

Healthcare, schools & county offices

  • Primary care, Bayfield: CommonSpirit Primary Care Bayfield (formerly Mercy Family Medicine — locals may still call it that), 480 Wolverine Dr Suite 3, (970) 764-9150.
  • Kids: Pediatric Partners of the Southwest has Bayfield clinics.
  • Emergency: the nearest ER is Mercy Hospital in Durango (~45 min), (970) 247-4311 — in any emergency call 911. Full list: emergency & lake contacts.
  • Schools: Bayfield School District 10 (see the year-round guide).
  • County offices: Assessor and Clerk at 679 Turner Dr, Durango.
