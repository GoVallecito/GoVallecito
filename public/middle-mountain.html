Explore · Middle Mountain (FR 724)

Middle Mountain

The lake's backyard mountain: about 11 miles of road, ATV loops, a karst-wilderness hike, and a 1920s ghost camp — here's how to do it right.

⚠️ Know before you ride
  • Trailer up — you can't ride there on the road. No La Plata County road (including CR 501) is open to OHVs; it's state law (C.R.S. 33-14.5-108), crossings only. See how to ride legal at Vallecito.
  • Register: a Colorado CPW OHV registration is $26.25/yr (Apr 1–Mar 31) — residents, out-of-staters, and plated rigs alike on designated trails (no out-of-state reciprocity).
  • Download the MVUM before you drive up. The Columbine District Motor Vehicle Use Map is the legal map of what's open to your vehicle class — and there's no cell service up there. Get the georeferenced PDF: fs.usda.gov/media/73572. Riding off designated routes is a federal violation.
  • Spark arrester required; Colorado limits OHV sound (96 dB on new machines). Kids 10–15 on designated trails only under direct visual supervision; DOT helmet guidance for under-18.
  • Season ~June 1 – Nov 30 (winter gate). Open-range cattle and cattle guards; storms build fast above 10,000 ft. Everything past the designated routes is Weminuche Wilderness — no motors or bikes, period.
  • Current conditions/gate: Columbine Ranger District (970) 884-2512 (walk-in Fridays). The Oct-2025 flood damaged roads forest-wide — check SJNF conditions.

Why go up

The destinations

⛏️ Tuckerville — the ghost camp

Born of a 1913 copper-and-silver rush in the Cave Basin Mining District ("a five-foot vein of good copper and galena"), Tuckerville was busiest in the 1920s — six bunkhouses, a shop, a mess hall — and abandoned by 1929 when the ore never paid. Every building is gone; what remains is an overlook above the cliff mines, some foundations, and the story. Look, don't take — the artifacts are federally protected (ARPA). Tuckerville sits just over the line in Hinsdale County (about 10,600 ft, near Runlett Peak). Local legend ties the rush to tales of Ute elder Old Weaselskin's lost gold — fun to repeat as legend, nothing more.

Nerd fact: Tuckers Tunnel (~1 mile south, ~11,000 ft) is the world type locality of the mineral theisite — first described to science from this obscure prospect (mindat.org).

🌾 Runlett Park & the overlooks

A vast high meadow under the Runlett Peak / Granite Peak sightlines; the road's upper bends give the big look back down on the reservoir. The Cave Basin / Dollar Lake overlook is arguably the best non-technical viewpoint in the district.

🍂 Fall color

Beri's aspens are the Forest Service's own selling line — late September is the show. Fall is also hunting season up here, so expect more traffic and wear blaze orange off the machine.

Not just machines

Horses & hikers

Cave Basin #530 (foot/horse only) and Vallecito View #808 both allow stock — Cave Basin is the quiet Wilderness option, Vallecito View shares the tread with machines in season. On the motorized routes, everyone shares the trail: uphill traffic and stock have the right of way, and a friendly wave goes a long way. At the Wilderness boundary the rules change hard — no motors, no bikes, no mechanized anything. See our trail guide and wildlife guide (you'll likely meet cattle, and maybe a moose).

Where to stage

Basecamp & services

Middle Mountain Campground sits at the bottom, on the Pine River inlet (24 lakefront sites) — the natural basecamp. It's reservable on recreation.gov, about $28–32/night, open late May–September, max RV 35 ft, with potable water and no horse facilities.

There's no formal trailhead lot at the bottom of FR 724 — but Altitude ATV & Side-by-Side Rentals is based at the bottom of Middle Mountain Road (32 Middle Mountain Rd, (970) 403-2260) and offers park-and-ride rentals and trailering. Up top there are no services, no water, and no cell — fuel up, fill up, and download your map before you leave the pavement.

🏔️ Old-timer's rule of thumb

Top off the gas, air down a touch for the rocks, and tell someone your plan and your turnaround time. There's no bar of signal to call for a tow once you're past the cattle guard.
