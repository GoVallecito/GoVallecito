A community hub for Vallecito Lake

Independently owned, locally operated, and written by people who actually live up here in the high country.

Why this site exists

Vallecito is a small lake town surrounded by big country — the edge of the Weminuche Wilderness, one road in, and weather that does what it wants. The information visitors and locals actually need — how full is the lake, is there a fire restriction, is the road clear, who do I call — was scattered across a dozen agency websites. We pulled it into one place.

What we do

  • Live conditions — marina weather, lake level, wildfire and fire-restriction status, streamflow, and roads, refreshed every 15 minutes from official sources.
  • A real local guide — the lodges, guides, restaurants, and shops that make up the community.
  • Practical know-how — trails, fishing, trip planning, and the small stuff you only learn by living here.

How we keep it honest

Conditions come straight from the National Weather Service, USGS, NIFC, USBR, and CDOT — we don't editorialize the data. Fire restrictions are set by the agencies that issue them. Business listings are free, and we fix mistakes fast: if something's wrong or out of date, tell us and we'll sort it.

Independent & local

GoVallecito.com isn't run by a chamber, a government, or an out-of-town media company. It's a local project, made with care in the San Juans. Featured partners (like our real-estate partner) help keep it free — and they're local too.

Questions, corrections, or want to get involved? Get in touch. We read everything.
